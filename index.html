Research

I am broadly interested in the mathematical foundations of machine learning. More specifically, I am interested in statistical machine learning, probabilistic modeling, and non-convex optimization. The interplay of mathematics and algorithms to answer questions like robustness, explainability and fairness in AI is exciting. Check out these amazing blogs to read more about the above areas - Off the Convex Path and ML@CMU.

Low Rank Joint Probability Tensor Recovery From One-Dimensional Marginals
Guide: Prof. Ajit Rajwade, Department of Computer Science, IIT Bombay
Report  /  Slides

Random Variables in real-world data are reasonably (in)dependent which induces a low-rank on the joint probability tensor and it thus follows the canonical polyadic decomposition. We can then use this prior to estimate the joint density function from just 1-D marginals in the Radon Space by taking random projections of the data.

Plug and Play Multimodal Generative Modeling with Tractable Inference
Guide: Prof. Guy Van den Broeck, Antonio Vergari, StarAI Lab, UCLA
To be updated soon

We can learn the joint distribution of various modalities by training independent autoencoders for each of them. The latent spaces of these can be synced by training a probabilistic circuit(sum product network) on the top of them which allows for tractable exact inference for a variety of queries.

Learning Mixture of Log-Concave Densities from Random Projections
Guide: Prof. Vivek Borkar, Department of Electrical Engineering, IIT Bombay
Report  /  Slides

High Dimensional distributions are believed to lie along underlying subspaces or manifolds. This work uses ideas of subspace clustering and random projections to prove theoretically that random images of a mixture of log-concave densities is Gaussian mixutre in the subspaces that can be learned efficiently.

Blind Calibration of Gains and Sensing Matrix Perturbations in Compressed Sensing
Guide: Prof. Ajit Rajwade, Department of Computer Science, IIT Bombay
Paper  /  Supplemental  /  Code  /  Under Review at ICASSP 2021

Compressed Sensing has gained popularity mainly because of its efficient hardware implementation. However, most sensing systems are miscalibrated due to sensor gains or in the sensing matrix itself. For MRI acquisition, these may arise due to a person moving or gradient delays in the machine. This work recovers the signal and the perturbations simultaneously from the measurements blindly.

Sequential Tests and Confidence Sequences for Prediction of Election Results
Guide: Prof. Shivaram Kalyankrishnan, Department of Computer Science, IIT Bombay,
Research Proposal

Predicting the winner of an election from limited samples of data in the form of polls is a fundamental problem of interest. Our work models polling as a community sampling-with- replacement model, and we investigate the sample complexity of queries needed to ensure a fixed mistake probability. The core of this work relies on sequential tests to ensure fixed mistake probability bounds.

Spatio-Temporal Activity Detection and Classification in Untrimmed Videos
Guide: Dr. Martin Klingkit, Hitachi Central Research Lab, Tokyo,
Paper  /  Trecvid Competition Page

Most of the work on action detection has been on trimmed and center cropped videos around the object doing the action. This work uses a pipeline of object detection, tracking followed by classification using the Temporal Reasoning Network. It introduces a event-based finite state machine to get a better temporal alignment of the activities using a simple sliding-window approach.

Conditional StyleGAN for Style Transfer in Audio
Course: Advanced Machine Learning, Prof. Amit Sethi
Report  /  Code

We modify the current StyleGAN architecture to incorporate class conditioning. We then use StyleGAN for style transfer in audio signals by converting them into time-frequency plots called mel spectrograms. It is tested on the Speech Commands dataset both in the unconditional and conditional settings.

Finding Shortest Path in a Maze
Course: Reinforcement Learning, Prof. Shivaram Kalyankrishnan
Report  /  Code

Modelled a 2D maze as an MDP with appropriate states, actions, rewards and transition probabilities and found the optimal policy using Howard’s Policy Iteration, Value Iteration and Linear Programming.
